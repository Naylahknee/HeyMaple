Here’s a clean, direct list of the major project types universities use at the undergraduate and graduate level. This covers the full landscape so you can compare formats across programs.

Undergraduate-Level Projects

1. Capstone Project – Applied, practical, or research-based final project.


2. Senior Thesis – Long-form academic research paper.


3. Senior Project – Creative or professional project (film, design, engineering build, etc.).


4. Portfolio – Collection of work demonstrating competency (arts, design, communications).


5. Practicum – Hands-on professional experience with reflective analysis.


6. Internship + Final Report – Work placement combined with a written evaluation or presentation.


7. Research Project – Independent or faculty-supervised study.


8. Group/Team Project – Collaborative project often tied to a capstone course.


9. Applied Project – Solving a real-world problem for an organization or industry partner.


10. Clinical Experience – Required in fields like nursing, education, counseling.


11. Field Study/Fieldwork – Data collection or observational research done on-site.


12. Performance or Production Project – Theater, film, dance, music programs.


13. Design Project – Engineering, architecture, UX, product design.


14. Service-Learning Project – Community-based project with academic integration.


15. Comprehensive Exam (Undergrad) – Less common, but used in some majors instead of a project.



Graduate-Level Projects (Master’s & Doctoral)

1. Master’s Thesis – Research-based, original academic contribution.


2. PhD Dissertation – Large-scale, original research advancing a field.


3. Capstone (Graduate) – Applied professional project demonstrating mastery.


4. Applied Research Project (ARP) – Common in public admin, education, social work.


5. Professional Project – Industry-oriented deliverable (business plan, campaign, product build).


6. Portfolio (Graduate) – Curated body of advanced work; common in arts, design, education.


7. Clinical Practicum – Required in counseling, psychology, social work, medical fields.


8. Residency Project – Medicine, PA, NP, and some arts MFA programs.


9. Case Study Analysis – MBA, MPA, policy, and applied social sciences.


10. Action Research Project – Especially in education and organizational leadership programs.


11. Comprehensive Exam (Graduate) – Sometimes required instead of or in addition to a thesis.


12. Qualifying Exam – Doctoral-level test required before dissertation research.


13. Field Research / Fieldwork Report – Anthropology, environmental science, journalism.


14. Creative Dissertation – MFA programs (novel, screenplay, poetry collection).


15. Studio Project – Architecture, urban planning, fine arts.


16. Lab-Based Research Project – STEM and biomedical fields.


17. Internship + Graduate-Level Analysis – Required for MBA, MPP, MPA, etc.


18. Teaching Practicum – Required for doctoral candidates who teach.


19. Policy Analysis Project – Public policy and social science programs.


20. Product Prototype + Documentation – Engineering, HCI, design, media innovation.


21. Roundtable or Symposium Presentation – Required in some professional programs.
